Brain Tumor Classification and Analysis Using ML Techniques

This project explores the use of AI and machine learning to enhance brain tumor detection, aiming to improve accuracy and early diagnosis through advanced image processing techniques. How can machine learning models improve the accuracy and efficiency of medical diagnoses? What role do image processing and feature extraction play in developing robust diagnostic tools?
Want to know these answers for the questions,then scroll down to know more 👇
Steps Involved in Creating the Project:
1) Gathered MRI scan images from medical repositories and Kaggle datasets.
2) Utilized Python (Pandas, NumPy) to compile and organize the data. Handled missing values, noise, and inconsistencies in the images
3) Conducted descriptive statistics to understand the distribution of tumor sizes and locations.
4) After analyzing the data we need to understand that the data is in the form of images. So, we need to filter the images by using filtering techniques like mean/median filtering. The noise will be removed after the filtering
5) Once the data is filtered then we can able to do segmentation in order to identify the brain tumor. This can be done using VGG16/Resnet50 algorithms.
6) Classification can be done based on the segmented MRI image. Key Takeaways from the Data:
1) Tumor Detection Accuracy: Achieved high accuracy in detecting and classifying various types of brain tumors.
2) Feature Importance: Identified critical features that significantly impact tumor classification, aiding in model interpretability.
3) Medical Insights: Gained valuable insights into tumor characteristics and diagnostic patterns, contributing to better medical understanding and treatment planning.

Filtering:
Most of the MRI images consists of more noise to remove that noise we are using Median filtering and mean filtering algorithms. Both the filtering algorithms used to remove noise from an image but the quality of the output image varies. Median filtering has high accuracy than mean filtering algorithm. So the quality of median filtered image is very high compared with mean filtered image.

Segmentation:
Segmentation is the process used to separate the image into different segments in the form of pixels. for segmentation purpose we are using VGG16 and ResNet50 algorithms. VGG16 is a object detection algorithm and it is used here for semantic segmentation purpose. It is used to enhance the performance of unet. It consists of 16 layers typically 13 convolutional layers, five Max Pooling layers, and three Dense layers but vgg16 contains only 16 weighted layers. ResNet50 is a convolutional neural network that contains 50 layers. In resnet50 we can train millions of images. Among both the segmentation algorithms VGG16 has high accuracy and it is used for classification of images too.

Classification:
After the segmentation the brain tumor image is classified based on the characteristics. For this purpose we are using ResNet50 and KNN algorithms. ResNet50 is a convolutional neural network that contains 50 layers. KNN algorithm is used to predict high accurate images. Based on the characteristics the segmented image can be classified into any type of brain tumors such as meningioma or glioma etc. Glioma affects the human a lot and it is very dangerous tumor whereas meningioma are benign but often recur after surgical removal.

Conclusion:
The "Unveiling the Future of Brain Tumor Detection: A Multi-faceted Approach Using AI &Machine Learning "project demonstrates the potential of advanced image processing and machine learning techniques to revolutionize medical diagnostics. Future enhancements could include integrating additional imaging modalities, expanding the model to detect other types of tumors, and incorporating more sophisticated AI algorithms to further improve diagnostic accuracy and efficiency. As the field of medical AI evolves, continuous innovation and collaboration with medical professionals will be essential to ensure the technology meets the highest standards of accuracy and reliability.
